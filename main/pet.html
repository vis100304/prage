GRAB AND GO: PET DISASTER TRAVEL KIT

Prepare a "Grab and GO" Kit

In the event of an emergency, having a kit packed and ready to go will help you evacuate quickly with your pet while ensuring they have everything they need to stay safe and comfortable. Here’s what to include:

Essentials:

Food and Water:

Pet Food: Pack at least 3 days' worth of non-perishable pet food. If your pet has dietary restrictions, be sure to include appropriate food.
Water: Bring bottled water (enough for 3 days). It's important to have clean water for your pet, as drinking from unfamiliar sources can lead to illness.
Collapsible Bowls:These are lightweight and easy to pack, making feeding and hydrating your pet on the go simple.

Collar,Harness, and Leash:

Collar/Harness: Ensure your pet wears a sturdy collar or harness with up-to-date ID tags attached. These tags should include your current phone number and any medical needs your pet has.
Leash: A strong leash for walking your pet is essential, especially in unfamiliar or chaotic environments.

Medications:

Prescriptions: Pack at least a week’s supply of any medications your pet requires. Ensure these are stored in a waterproof bag.
Medical Records: Include copies of your pet's medical records, including vaccination history and a list of any health issues. This information can be vital if your pet needs care while you're away from home.
First-Aid Kit:Include a basic pet-specific first-aid kit with bandages, antiseptic wipes, tweezers, and any necessary medications.

Fabric Muzzle:

Muzzle:If your pet becomes anxious or aggressive when stressed, pack a fabric muzzle. This is especially important if you anticipate being in close quarters with other animals or people.

Cleaning Supplies:

Waste Bags:Bring plenty of waste bags for dogs. For cats, pack a small, disposable litter box and litter.
Cleaning Supplies:Include paper towels, disinfectant wipes, and hand sanitizer to manage any messes and maintain hygiene.

Comfort Items:

Toy/Blanket: A favorite toy or blanket can help reduce your pet’s stress in a new environment.
Bedding: A small bed or towel gives your pet a familiar place to rest.

Identification and Documentation:

Photos:Keep recent photos of your pet in case you get separated. These will help others identify your pet.
Microchip Information: If your pet is microchipped, carry the information with you, including the microchip number and the contact details of the company that registered the chip.
